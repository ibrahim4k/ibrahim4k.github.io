Program: Toronto Metropolitan University - Business Technology Management (BComm)

This program is Canada's largest school that is dedicated to business management and communication technologies. It combines business and tech skills to teach students how to manage IT projects and allign technology with business goals. Graduates are able to pursue roles in various careers such as IT consulting, business analysis, tech management, and more.

Year 4 Fall Semester Courses:

  1. ITM 707 Strategy, Management, and Acquisition

    2. Students learn how to manage information systems to support organizational goals. This course focuses on IS strategy, acquisition, and development from a senior managers perspective.

  2. ITM 750 IS Project Management

    3. Student will learn techniques for project planning, budgeting, scheduling, quality, communication, and risk management. This course focuses on the industry-standard Project Management Body of Knowledge (PMBOK).

  3. ITM 780 Web Design and Management

    4. Students learn how to develop and manage business websites. This course focuses on HTML5, CSS3, and web standards.

  4. RTA 928 Introduction to Game Design

    5. This course introduces the core elements of game design, including game world, character, interactivity, and gameplay mechanics.

  5. SOC 902 Hollywood and Society

    6. This course explores Hollywood's cultural impact, focusing on its history, structure, celebrity culture, and globalization.

The course I have found most useful in this program so far is ITM 550: Advanced e-Business. It taught me how to effectively build and design websites. I applied these skills outside of school by redesigning my own e-commerce site. This site is presently operational and generates a modest profit.
